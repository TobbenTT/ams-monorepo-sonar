## 1. OR Definition and Core Principles

**Operational Readiness (OR)** is the systematic process of preparing an organization, its people, processes, systems, and infrastructure to safely and efficiently operate a new or modified industrial facility. OR ensures that everything required for Day 1 of operations is identified, planned, procured, installed, tested, and validated before commissioning.

### Core Principles

1. **Early Start**: OR activities must begin at the conceptual design phase (36-48 months before SOP Day), not during commissioning
2. **Systematic Approach**: Every OR workstream follows a structured methodology with defined deliverables, gates, and validation criteria
3. **Integration**: OR is not a standalone discipline but integrates with project management, engineering, construction, and commissioning
4. **Risk-Based Prioritization**: Resources are allocated based on operational risk and criticality
5. **Measurable Progress**: All OR activities have KPIs and gate criteria that enable objective progress measurement
6. **Knowledge Capture**: Lessons learned are captured and applied to future projects

### The Problem OR Solves

Industry data demonstrates the critical need for OR:
- 70% of megaprojects suffer delays of >12 months (McKinsey)
- >60% of plants start operations without complete SOPs (Deloitte)
- The "valley of death" between EPC handover and stable operations costs 5-15% of CAPEX (BCG)
- Reactive maintenance costs 3-5x more than planned maintenance (Gartner)
- 45% of industrial projects report workforce readiness as top concern (PwC)

## 2. OR Project Lifecycle Phases

### Phase 1: Conceptual / Pre-Feasibility (36-48 months before SOP)
- OR Strategy definition
- Initial risk assessment
- High-level OR budget estimation
- Stakeholder engagement plan
- Gate G0: OR Strategy Approved

### Phase 2: Feasibility / Basic Engineering (24-36 months before SOP)
- OR Plan 360 development
- Organizational design (preliminary)
- Staffing strategy
- Initial maintenance philosophy
- Regulatory landscape mapping
- Gate G1: OR Plan Approved

### Phase 3: Detailed Engineering (18-24 months before SOP)
- Detailed maintenance strategies (RCM/FMECA)
- SOP development begins
- Training needs analysis
- Contract strategy for O&M services
- Procurement of long-lead spares
- Systems readiness planning (CMMS, DCS, etc.)
- Gate G2: Detailed Plans Complete

### Phase 4: Construction / Pre-Commissioning (12-18 months before SOP)
- Staffing and recruitment execution
- Training delivery
- SOP completion and validation
- Maintenance plans loaded in CMMS
- Spare parts procurement and warehousing
- Safety systems verification
- Gate G3: Pre-Commissioning Ready

### Phase 5: Commissioning / Startup (0-6 months)
- Pre-Startup Safety Review (PSSR)
- System completions and handover (TCCC model)
- Performance testing
- Ramp-up execution
- Gate G4: Safe to Operate

### Phase 6: Post-Startup / Stabilization (0-12 months after SOP)
- Performance monitoring
- Ramp-up tracking
- Defect elimination
- Lessons learned capture
- Gate G5: Stable Operations Achieved

## 3. 6-Level OR Structure (LV1-LV6)

The OR program is structured in 6 hierarchical levels:

| Level | Name | Description | Owner |
|-------|------|-------------|-------|
| LV1 | OR Strategy | Overall OR approach, governance, budget | OR Director / Steering Committee |
| LV2 | OR Plan 360 | Comprehensive plan covering all workstreams | OR Manager |
| LV3 | Workstream Plans | Detailed plans per domain (Operations, Maintenance, HR, HSE, etc.) | Workstream Leads |
| LV4 | Activity Plans | Specific activity plans within each workstream | Activity Owners |
| LV5 | Deliverables | Individual documents, procedures, systems | Deliverable Owners |
| LV6 | Tasks | Execution tasks tracked in project schedule | Task Owners |

## 4. OR Governance Model

### Organizational Structure
- **Steering Committee**: Strategic direction, gate approvals, resource allocation
- **OR Manager**: Day-to-day management of the OR program
- **Workstream Leads**: Domain experts managing their area (Operations, Maintenance, HR, HSE, Finance, Legal, Procurement, IT/OT)
- **Document Controller**: Information management, version control, completeness tracking

### Decision Rights (RACI)
- **R (Responsible)**: The workstream lead who creates the deliverable
- **A (Accountable)**: The OR Manager who approves the deliverable
- **C (Consulted)**: Cross-functional stakeholders who provide input
- **I (Informed)**: Stakeholders who receive the deliverable

### Escalation Path
1. Workstream Lead identifies issue
2. Escalation to OR Manager if cross-functional
3. Escalation to Steering Committee if strategic/budget impact
4. Client escalation if information/decision required from asset owner

## 5. Gate Review Framework

### Gate Structure

| Gate | Name | Timing | Key Criteria |
|------|------|--------|-------------|
| G0 | OR Strategy Approved | Concept phase | Strategy document signed, budget allocated, governance established |
| G1 | OR Plan Approved | Feasibility | 360 plan complete, workstream leads assigned, schedule baselined |
| G2 | Detailed Plans Complete | Detailed engineering | All LV3 plans complete, recruitment underway, training plan approved |
| G3 | Pre-Commissioning Ready | Construction complete | All SOPs written, staff trained, CMMS loaded, spares in warehouse |
| G4 | Safe to Operate | Commissioning | PSSR complete, all systems verified, regulatory approvals obtained |
| G5 | Stable Operations | Post-startup | Performance targets met, ramp-up complete, defects eliminated |

### Gate Review Process
1. OR Manager prepares gate package (deliverable status, KPIs, risks, issues)
2. Workstream leads present their area status
3. Steering Committee reviews and decides: Pass / Conditional Pass / Fail
4. Action items tracked to closure
5. Gate outcome documented and communicated

## 6. OR KPIs and Performance Measurement

### Leading KPIs (Predictive)
| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| OR Deliverable Completion | >95% at each gate | Completed deliverables / Total planned |
| SOP Completion Rate | 100% by G3 | SOPs completed / SOPs required |
| Training Completion Rate | 100% by G4 | Staff trained / Staff required |
| Staffing Fill Rate | >90% by G3 | Positions filled / Positions required |
| CMMS Readiness | 100% by G3 | Master data loaded / Total master data |
| Spare Parts Availability | >95% by G4 | Critical spares received / Critical spares required |

### Lagging KPIs (Performance)
| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| Time to First Product | On schedule | Actual vs. planned first product date |
| Ramp-up Trajectory | >90% of plan | Actual production vs. ramp-up plan |
| Safety Incidents (TRIR) | 0 in first 6 months | Total recordable incident rate |
| Asset Availability | >92% within 6 months | Actual uptime / Planned uptime |
| Maintenance Backlog | <2 weeks within 3 months | Outstanding work orders in weeks |

## 7. OR Budget Methodology

### Budget Categories
1. **OR Management**: Program management, governance, reporting
2. **People**: Recruitment, relocation, training, temporary staff
3. **Processes**: SOP development, methodology, consulting
4. **Systems**: CMMS, ERP, DCS configuration, IT infrastructure
5. **Materials**: Spare parts, consumables, first fills
6. **Contracts**: O&M contracts, service agreements
7. **Facilities**: Temporary facilities, offices, workshops

### Budget Estimation Approaches
- **Top-Down**: Percentage of CAPEX (typically 3-8% depending on complexity)
- **Bottom-Up**: Detailed estimation by activity and resource
- **Benchmarking**: Comparison with similar projects in the same industry
- **Hybrid**: Top-down for initial estimate, bottom-up for detailed planning

## 8. OR Risk Management Framework

### Risk Categories
1. **Schedule Risk**: Delays in OR activities affecting commissioning date
2. **People Risk**: Inability to recruit or train sufficient qualified staff
3. **Technical Risk**: Equipment or systems not meeting performance requirements
4. **Regulatory Risk**: Permits or approvals delayed or denied
5. **Financial Risk**: Budget overruns or funding delays
6. **Interface Risk**: Poor coordination between EPC and operations

### Risk Assessment Methodology
- Use 5x5 risk matrix (Likelihood x Consequence)
- Assess across 5 consequence categories: Safety, Environment, Production, Financial, Reputation
- Identify both inherent risk (without controls) and residual risk (with controls)
- Apply bow-tie methodology for critical risks (threats → barriers → consequences)
- Reference VSC Failure Modes Table for equipment-related risks
