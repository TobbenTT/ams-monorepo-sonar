## 1. Capital Investment System (SIC)

**Source:** `SIC-M-001 Manual de Sistema de Inversion de Capital`

### Stage-Gate Process

| Stage | Gate | Key Activities | OR Integration |
|-------|------|---------------|----------------|
| Profile (Perfil) | G0 | Opportunity identification, pre-screening | OR awareness, initial stakeholder identification |
| Pre-Feasibility | G1 | Conceptual engineering, order of magnitude estimate | OR Strategy development, preliminary staffing |
| Feasibility | G2 | Basic engineering, +/-20% estimate | OR Plan 360, detailed workstream planning |
| Execution | G3/G4 | Detailed engineering, construction | OR execution, recruitment, training |
| Startup | G5 | Commissioning, ramp-up | OR validation, operational handover |

### Investment Decision Criteria
- Technical feasibility confirmed
- Economic viability (NPV, IRR) demonstrated
- Environmental and social impact assessed
- Operational readiness plan approved
- Risk profile acceptable

## 2. Project Stage-Gate Framework

### Profile Stage (SIC-P-002)
- Strategic alignment assessment
- Preliminary scope definition
- Order of magnitude cost estimate (+/-50%)
- Key risk identification
- Conceptual schedule
- **OR Deliverables**: None formal, but OR awareness begins

### Pre-Feasibility Stage (SIC-P-003)
- Conceptual engineering
- Preliminary process design
- Cost estimate (+/-30%)
- Environmental baseline studies begin
- **OR Deliverables**: OR Strategy (LV1), preliminary risk assessment

### Feasibility Stage (SIC-P-004)
- Basic engineering complete
- Cost estimate (+/-15-20%)
- Environmental Impact Assessment
- Permitting strategy
- Procurement strategy for long-lead items
- **OR Deliverables**: OR Plan 360 (LV2), all workstream plans (LV3), staffing strategy

## 3. Scope, Strategy, Schedule & Cost Estimation

**Source:** `PCOE.GLB.STD.0003 Standard Scope Strategy Schedule and Cost Estimating Classifications`

### Estimation Classes (AACE International)

| Class | Maturity Level | Purpose | Accuracy Range |
|-------|---------------|---------|---------------|
| Class 5 | 0-2% engineering | Screening, concept | -50% to +100% |
| Class 4 | 1-15% engineering | Pre-feasibility | -30% to +50% |
| Class 3 | 10-40% engineering | Feasibility, budget authorization | -20% to +30% |
| Class 2 | 30-75% engineering | Control, bid evaluation | -15% to +20% |
| Class 1 | 65-100% engineering | Definitive, execution | -10% to +15% |

### Cost Structure for OR Budget Integration
- **Direct Costs**: Equipment, materials, construction labor
- **Indirect Costs**: Engineering, project management, temporary facilities
- **Owner's Costs**: Staff, training, spare parts, O&M preparation (includes OR)
- **Contingency**: Risk-based contingency calculation

### Schedule Development
- Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) alignment with OR activities
- Critical path analysis including OR milestones
- Resource loading for OR team
- Integration milestones between construction and OR

## 4. Services Requirements for Projects

**Source:** `capital-projects-services-requirements.pdf`

### Service Categories Relevant to OR
- Operational readiness consulting
- Maintenance engineering services
- Training and development services
- Organizational design services
- HSE management services
- Document management services
- Systems integration services (CMMS, ERP)

### Service Delivery Models
| Model | Description | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Time & Materials (T&M) | Hourly rates, flexible scope | Early phases, scope uncertain |
| Fixed Price | Defined scope, fixed fee | Well-defined deliverables |
| Cost Plus | Actual costs + margin | Complex, integrated programs |
| Outcome-Based | Payment tied to results | Performance-oriented engagements |
