# Salares Norte -- Industry Benchmarks

This document surveys comparable high-altitude mining operations and applicable standards that inform the maintenance strategy for Salares Norte. The goal is to leverage lessons learned from similar environments rather than relying solely on OEM sea-level recommendations.

---

## 1. Comparable High-Altitude Mines

### 1.1 Maricunga Mine (4,200 -- 4,500 m ASL)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | Atacama Region, Chile (same district as Salares Norte) |
| Owner | Kinross Gold (formerly Bema Gold) |
| Elevation | 4,200 -- 4,500 m |
| Type | Open-pit gold heap leach |
| Climate | Virtually identical to Salares Norte |
| Status | Operations suspended; closure/remediation phase |

**Maintenance Lessons:**
- Maricunga was one of the first modern high-altitude gold mines in Chile and pioneered many cold-weather protocols that Salares Norte can adopt.
- **Heat tracing was found to be non-negotiable** -- even "redundant" circuits failed in extreme cold events.
- Heap leach solution froze repeatedly in early years; recirculation and insulation were retrofitted.
- Diesel fleet performance: CAT engines required **altitude kits and turbocharger upgrades** to approach reasonable productivity.
- Workforce rotation of **7x7 or 4x4** was found necessary to manage altitude fatigue; longer rotations led to increased error rates.
- Elastomer degradation (pump diaphragms, conveyor belts) was 2-3x faster than Kinross's lower-altitude operations.

### 1.2 Cerro Casale / Norte Abierto (4,000 -- 4,600 m ASL)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | Atacama Region, Chile (~20 km from Salares Norte) |
| Owner | Newmont / Barrick (JV) |
| Elevation | 4,000 -- 4,600 m |
| Type | Proposed open-pit gold-copper (pre-feasibility) |
| Climate | Virtually identical to Salares Norte |
| Status | Development stage (Norte Abierto JV) |

**Maintenance Lessons (from feasibility studies):**
- Norte Abierto engineering studies confirmed the need for **all-synthetic lubricant programs** site-wide.
- Feasibility-level maintenance cost estimates included a **30% altitude uplift** over comparable sea-level operations.
- Power supply studies confirmed diesel derating of ~25% and recommended hybrid solar to reduce fuel logistics.
- Material handling studies specified **high-chrome wear parts** for all ore-contact surfaces.
- The proximity to Salares Norte means shared supply chain and logistics corridors -- potential for spare parts pooling.

### 1.3 Pascua-Lama (>4,500 m ASL)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | Atacama Region, Chile / San Juan, Argentina (border) |
| Owner | Barrick Gold |
| Elevation | 4,500 -- 5,200 m (highest areas above 5,000 m) |
| Type | Proposed open-pit gold-silver |
| Climate | More extreme than Salares Norte (higher, colder, more wind) |
| Status | Construction suspended; environmental and legal challenges |

**Maintenance Lessons (from construction and engineering phases):**
- Pascua-Lama construction experience revealed that **worker productivity at >4,500 m is approximately 50-60% of sea level** for heavy mechanical tasks.
- Equipment commissioning took significantly longer than planned due to altitude effects on both equipment and people.
- The project designed a **fully enclosed process plant** to protect equipment and workers from weather -- a design principle not fully adopted at Salares Norte.
- Conveyor systems through the cross-border tunnel required **zero-maintenance sections** in inaccessible areas, driving redundancy requirements.
- Steel specification: all structural steel was **impact-tested at -40C** (more conservative than typical -30C).
- Barrick developed proprietary cold-start procedures for large diesel engines above 4,000 m that are now industry reference documents.

### 1.4 El Indio Mine (3,800 -- 4,200 m ASL, Historical)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | Coquimbo Region, Chile |
| Owner | Barrick Gold (formerly LAC Minerals, St. Joe Minerals) |
| Elevation | 3,800 -- 4,200 m |
| Type | Underground gold-silver-copper (historical) |
| Climate | Similar to Salares Norte but slightly lower and warmer |
| Status | Closed (1990-2002 operational period) |

**Maintenance Lessons (historical record):**
- El Indio operated for 12+ years at high altitude and built one of the first comprehensive altitude-adjustment maintenance databases in Chilean mining.
- **Lubricant changeover protocols** (summer/winter grades) were formalized at El Indio and became standard practice for Atacama/Coquimbo operations.
- Underground operations protected equipment from UV and wind, but portal areas and surface facilities showed the same accelerated degradation seen at open-pit sites.
- El Indio's experience demonstrated that **seal replacement frequency must be doubled** vs sea-level standards for any rotating equipment exposed to thermal cycling.
- The mine's closure plan provides data on long-term structural steel degradation at altitude (20+ years of exposure data).

### 1.5 Chuquicamata (2,850 m ASL, Reference Benchmark)

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | Antofagasta Region, Chile |
| Owner | Codelco |
| Elevation | 2,850 m (lower than Salares Norte, but relevant) |
| Type | Open-pit (transitioning to underground) copper |
| Climate | Extreme aridity, high UV, but warmer than Salares Norte |
| Status | Operational (underground transition) |

**Maintenance Lessons (reference operation):**
- Chuquicamata is the **best-documented large-scale maintenance operation** in Chilean high-altitude mining.
- At 2,850 m, diesel derating is ~12-15% -- significantly less than Salares Norte's 25-30%.
- Chuquicamata's maintenance intervals can be used as a **baseline with additional correction** for the 1,650 m altitude difference.
- Codelco's RCM program at Chuquicamata established **criticality-based maintenance** frameworks that became standard across Chilean mining.
- Silica abrasion data from Chuquicamata's oxide and sulphide ore processing provides relevant wear-rate comparisons.
- UV degradation rates at Chuquicamata are documented but slightly lower than Salares Norte due to the 1,650 m altitude difference (~15% less UV).

## 3. Applicable Standards and Frameworks

### 3.1 Asset Management Framework

| Standard | Application at Salares Norte |
|---|---|
| **ISO 55000:2014** -- Asset Management Overview | Overarching framework for the AMS (Asset Management System). Defines policy, strategy, plans, and implementation. |
| **ISO 55001:2014** -- Requirements | Certification-level requirements. Gold Fields corporate asset management philosophy aligns with ISO 55001. |
| **ISO 55002:2018** -- Guidelines | Implementation guidance. Used to structure the SAMP (Strategic Asset Management Plan). |

### 3.2 Reliability and Maintenance Methodology

| Standard | Application at Salares Norte |
|---|---|
| **SAE JA-1011** -- RCM Evaluation Criteria | Defines the seven questions of RCM analysis. Used as the basis for the failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA) in the maintenance strategy. |
| **SAE JA-1012** -- RCM Guide | Implementation guide for JA-1011. Provides the methodology for task selection logic. |
| **ISO 14224:2016** -- Reliability and Maintenance Data | Defines the equipment taxonomy (9-level hierarchy), failure mode coding, and data collection standards. The SAP PM equipment hierarchy and notification coding at Salares Norte must align with ISO 14224. |

### 3.3 Risk-Based Inspection

| Standard | Application at Salares Norte |
|---|---|
| **API 580** -- Risk-Based Inspection (General) | Framework for prioritizing inspection resources based on probability and consequence of failure. |
| **API 581** -- RBI Methodology | Quantitative RBI methodology. Applied to pressure vessels, piping, and tanks in the process plant. |
| **API 510** -- Pressure Vessel Inspection | In-service inspection of pressure vessels. Applies to leach tanks, Merrill-Crowe equipment. |
| **API 570** -- Piping Inspection | In-service inspection of piping. Critical for slurry lines and cyanide circuits. |
| **API 653** -- Tank Inspection | In-service inspection of storage tanks. Applied to all atmospheric tanks. |

### 3.4 Electrical Testing

| Standard | Application at Salares Norte |
|---|---|
| **NETA MTS** -- Maintenance Testing Specifications | Defines test procedures and frequencies for electrical equipment maintenance. Applied to all switchgear, transformers, protective relays, and cables. |
| **NETA ATS** -- Acceptance Testing Specifications | Used during commissioning and after major electrical maintenance. |
| **IEC 60034-1** -- Rotating Electrical Machines | Altitude derating tables for electric motors. Motors above 4,000 m require specific altitude rating. |
| **IEC 62271** -- High-Voltage Switchgear | Altitude correction factors for dielectric withstand. All MV/HV switchgear must be rated for 4,500 m. |
| **IEEE 43** -- Insulation Resistance Testing | Motor winding insulation test procedures. Critical for condensation-prone environment. |

### 3.5 Vibration and Condition Monitoring

| Standard | Application at Salares Norte |
|---|---|
| **ISO 10816** -- Vibration Evaluation | Vibration severity criteria for rotating machinery. Used to set alarm and trip levels for all rotating equipment. |
| **ISO 13373** -- Condition Monitoring | Framework for condition monitoring and diagnostics. |
| **ISO 18436** -- Condition Monitoring Certification | Certification requirements for vibration analysts and condition monitoring technicians. |

### 3.6 Structural and Materials

| Standard | Application at Salares Norte |
|---|---|
| **ASTM A572** -- High-Strength Low-Alloy Steel | Specifies structural steel grades with Charpy impact testing at low temperatures. |
| **ASME BPVC Section VIII** -- Pressure Vessels | Design and fabrication of pressure vessels with fatigue analysis for thermal cycling service. |

## 4. Gold Fields Corporate Asset Management Philosophy

Gold Fields Limited operates under a corporate asset management framework that provides site-level guidance:

### Key Corporate Principles

1. **Life-of-Mine perspective**: All asset decisions consider total cost of ownership over the remaining mine life (11.5 years for Salares Norte), not just immediate maintenance cost.

2. **Risk-based prioritization**: Maintenance resources are allocated based on a formal risk assessment that considers safety, environmental, production, and cost consequences.

3. **Standardized equipment taxonomy**: Gold Fields uses a standardized equipment classification system across all operations, enabling benchmarking between sites (Ghana, South Africa, Australia, Peru, Chile).

4. **SAP PM as the enterprise platform**: All Gold Fields operations use SAP Plant Maintenance as the CMMS, with standardized master data structures (functional locations, equipment, BOMs, task lists, maintenance plans).

5. **Continuous improvement through data**: OEE, MTBF, MTTR, and cost-per-tonne KPIs are tracked and benchmarked across the portfolio. Salares Norte data will be compared against Cerro Corona (Peru, 3,900 m) and other high-altitude operations.

6. **Contractor management**: Where mining is contractor-operated (ICV at Salares Norte), Gold Fields maintains oversight of fleet maintenance through KPIs embedded in the mining contract.

### Salares Norte Adaptation

The corporate framework must be adapted for Salares Norte's extreme conditions:

| Corporate Standard | Salares Norte Adaptation |
|---|---|
| OEM-recommended intervals | Reduced by 25-30% (altitude/environment factor) |
| Standard material specs | Cold-rated, UV-resistant, abrasion-resistant specifications |
| Normal shutdown scheduling | Restricted to Oct-Mar window |
| Standard spare parts holding | Increased insurance stock due to remoteness |
| Standard workforce productivity | 30-40% productivity adjustment for altitude |
