## 1. OR Playbook V4 Overview

**Source:** `or-playbook--250522_OR PLAYBOOK V4.pdf`

### Playbook Purpose
The OR Playbook is the comprehensive operational document that defines HOW to execute an OR program. Unlike the Strategy (which defines WHAT and WHY), the Playbook provides step-by-step procedures, templates, and tools for each OR activity.

### Playbook Structure
| Chapter | Content |
|---------|---------|
| 1. Introduction | Purpose, scope, how to use the playbook |
| 2. OR Framework | Governance model, stage-gate process, RACI |
| 3. People Readiness | Staffing, recruitment, training, competency |
| 4. Process Readiness | SOPs, operating model, ramp-up planning |
| 5. Physical Asset Readiness | Maintenance strategy, CMMS, spare parts |
| 6. Safety & Environment Readiness | HSE systems, permits, emergency response |
| 7. Systems Readiness | IT/OT, CMMS, ERP, DCS configuration |
| 8. Financial Readiness | OPEX budget, working capital, insurance |
| 9. Supply Chain Readiness | Contracts, procurement, warehousing |
| 10. Commissioning Integration | Commissioning plan, PSSR, system turnover |
| 11. Ramp-Up & Stabilization | Production ramp-up, performance monitoring |
| 12. Templates & Tools | Document templates, checklists, forms |

### Playbook vs. Strategy vs. Plan

| Document | When Created | Scope | Audience | Evolves |
|----------|-------------|-------|----------|---------|
| OR Strategy | Phase 1 (Concept) | Program-level | Steering Committee | Rarely |
| OR Plan 360 | Phase 2 (Feasibility) | Project-specific | Project Team | Per phase |
| OR Playbook | Phase 2-3 | HOW-TO guide | OR Team | Continuously |

## 2. OR Corporate Strategy (INNOVX)

**Source:** `V.8 INNOVX's OR STRATEGY PRESENTATION [low].pdf`

### Corporate OR Strategy Elements
This presentation represents a corporate-level OR strategy (as opposed to project-level). Key elements:

1. **Corporate OR Vision**: Why OR is a strategic capability for the organization
2. **OR Capability Model**: Maturity progression from project-by-project to enterprise capability
3. **OR Portfolio Management**: Managing OR across multiple concurrent projects
4. **OR Standards**: Corporate standards that all projects must follow
5. **OR Talent Development**: Building an OR center of excellence
6. **Continuous Improvement**: Lessons learned from project to project

### Value for VSC
- Demonstrates the difference between project OR and corporate OR capability
- Shows how to position VSC as a strategic OR partner (not just project execution)
- Template for client presentations on OR value proposition

## 5. OR and Asset Integrity Integration

**Source:** `or-and-asset-integrity-procedure-MAM-PRJ00-TD-PRC-00020.pdf`

### Asset Integrity Management (AIM) in OR Context
Asset Integrity Management ensures that assets are designed, installed, maintained, and operated in a way that maintains their structural and functional integrity throughout their lifecycle.

### Integration Points with OR
| OR Workstream | Asset Integrity Element |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Maintenance Strategy | Integrity-critical maintenance tasks, inspection plans |
| Operating Procedures | Operating envelope limits, integrity monitoring |
| Risk Assessment | Integrity failure modes in risk register |
| HSE | Safety-critical element identification |
| Commissioning | Integrity verification during system handover |
| Training | Integrity awareness and competency |

### Key Deliverables
- Safety and Environmentally Critical Elements (SECE) register
- Integrity management plan
- Performance standards for safety barriers
- Inspection and monitoring schedule
- Competency requirements for integrity-related roles
